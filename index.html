Examination Trends & Priority Analysis

Based on 5 Years of Papers (2018-2024). Focus on Critical topics first.

Module I: Matrices
  • Critical (100%)
    Cayley-Hamilton Theorem: Verify the equation & Find Inverse. This appears in almost every paper's Section B.
  • Critical
    Rank of Matrix (Normal Form): Specifically reducing a 4x4 matrix to Normal Form. The exact same matrix often repeats.
  • High Yield
    Consistency of Linear Equations: Finding values of $\lambda$ and $\mu$ for No/Unique/Infinite solutions.
  • Medium
    Eigenvalues/Eigenvectors: Standard calculation questions, usually $3\times3$ matrices.
Module II: Differential Calculus
  • Critical
    Leibniz Theorem: Finding the $n$-th derivative at $x=0$. Common functions: $\log(x^2)$, $\sin^{-1}(x)$, $\tan^{-1}(x)$.
  • High Yield
    Euler's Theorem: Verification for Homogeneous functions. Often involves partial differentiation.
  • High Yield
    Jacobian: Checking for Functional Dependence between $u, v, w$.
  • Medium
    Series Expansion: Maclaurin/Taylor series expansion of functions like $e^x \sin y$.
Module III: Multiple Integrals
  • Critical (100%)
    Change of Order of Integration: This is a guaranteed question. You MUST draw the region to get marks.
  • High Yield
    Beta & Gamma Functions: Proofs like $\Gamma(1/2)=\sqrt{\pi}$ and simple evaluations. Easy 2-mark questions.
  • Medium
    Dirichlet's Integral: Applications to Area and Volume (e.g., Cylindrical column, Ellipsoid mass).
Module IV: Vector Calculus
  • Critical
    Integral Theorems: Verify/Evaluate Gauss Divergence, Stoke's, or Green's Theorem. The "Triangle" and "Cube" boundary problems are standard.
  • High Yield
    Directional Derivative: Finding D.D. in the direction of a line or vector. Very common in Section C.
  • Medium
    Irrotational/Solenoidal: Finding constants $a, b$ to make a vector irrotational (Curl = 0).

These questions have appeared identically or with minor number changes in multiple years. Mastering these ensures passing marks.

Matrices (100% Certainty)

Rank by Normal Form

Question: Find the rank of matrix by reducing to Normal Form:

$$A=\begin{bmatrix}1&2&-1&3\\ 4&1&2&1\\ 3&-1&1&2\\ 1&2&0&1\end{bmatrix}$$

Sources: 2018-19, 2023-24

Matrices (High Prob)

Consistency Parameters

Question: Find $\lambda$ and $\mu$ for solution types:

$$2x+3y+5z=9$$ $$7x+3y-2z=8$$ $$2x+3y+\lambda z=\mu$$

Sources: 2022-23, 2023-24

Diff Calc (Exact Repeat)

Lagrange's Multipliers

Question: Find volume of largest rectangular parallelopiped inscribed in ellipsoid:

$$\frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} + \frac{z^2}{c^2} = 1$$

Sources: 2018-19, 2022-23 SCOP

Vector Calc (Exact Repeat)

Green's Theorem (Triangle)

Question: Evaluate $\int_C (x^2y dx + x^2 dy)$ around triangle with vertices $(0,0), (1,0), (1,1)$.

Sources: 2018-19, 2023-24

Integration (Concept Repeat)

Change of Order

Question: Change order and evaluate:

$$I = \int_{0}^{a}\int_{y}^{a} \frac{x}{x^2+y^2} dx dy$$

(Or variations like $\int_0^1 \int_x^{2-x}$)

Sources: ALL 5 Years have this type.

Diff Calc (Recurring)

Jacobian

Question: If $x+y+z=u, y+z=uv, z=uvw$, show Jacobian is $u^2v$.

Sources: 2018-19, 2023-24 (similar logic)

Diff Calc (Recurring)

Leibniz Theorem

Question: Find nth derivative of $\log(x^2)$ or $\log(3x^2+x^3)$.

Sources: 2022-23, 2023-24

Vector Calc (Recurring)

Directional Derivative

Pattern: Find D.D. of $\phi$ at point $P$ in direction of line $PQ$.

Sources: 2018, 2019, 2023
